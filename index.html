Happy Tail's Redesign Case Study

Our challenge was to redesign a non-profit organization's website. The organization we decided on was Happy Tailâ€™s Rescue, an organization dedicated to saving animals, something I am very passionate about.

Summary

PROBLEM

The organization's current website has an outdated style, is not mobile-friendly, and does not convey the trust needed for someone to choose that organization to adopt from, foster from, or donate to.

SOLUTION

We would create a redesign that would convey a dependable and inviting experience to encourage an increase in adoption and foster applicants by building a trust with the user while also highlighting the purpose and actions of the organization to appeal to a wider user audience and potential donors.

MY ROLE & RESPONSIBILITIES

My role was as a UX researcher and UI designer. We worked in a team of four, but due to some participation issues the redesign was mainly done by one other person and myself. I helped work through all pieces of the process from research, ideation, definition, wireframes and designing to creating high-fidelity prototypes.

DELIVERABLES

Our deliveriables consisted of a high-fidelity prototype of a desktop website as well as a responsive mobile version of our redesign. The pages that made up our prorotype were based on the four pages we thought our user persona would use to complete their main goal of adopting a cat through the organization.

Phase 1 - Research

In this portion of the project we first made assumptions about our user by creating our proto-persona and empathy map. We then did research to back up and prove our assumptions in the form of a survey and 6 user interview, which we analyzed in the form of an affinity diagram and 2 x 2 priority matrix.
